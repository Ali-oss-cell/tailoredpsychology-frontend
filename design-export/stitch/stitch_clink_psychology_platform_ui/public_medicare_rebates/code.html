Understanding Medicare Rebates

Psychological care should be accessible. The Australian Government provides Medicare rebates for psychological services under a Mental Health Care Plan to help cover the costs of your sessions.

How it works arrow_downward

Are you eligible?

To claim a rebate, you must have a valid Mental Health Care Plan (MHCP) issued by a general practitioner (GP), psychiatrist, or paediatrician before your session.

clinical_notes

The MHCP

A Mental Health Care Plan allows you to claim up to 10 individual psychological appointments per calendar year.

payments

Rebate Amount

The current Medicare rebate is $93.35 for a Registered Psychologist and $137.05 for a Clinical Psychologist per 50-minute session.

event_repeat

Session Limits

Sessions are usually referred in blocks (e.g., initially 6 sessions). You will need a review with your GP to access the remaining 4 sessions.

How to claim your rebate

1

Visit your GP

Book a long consultation with your GP to discuss your mental health. Ask them to prepare a Mental Health Care Plan (MHCP) and provide a referral letter addressed to Clink Psychology Clinic.

2

Send us your referral

Before your first appointment, email your MHCP and referral letter to our admin team. We need this on file to process your rebates automatically.

3

Attend and Pay

Attend your session. Following the session, you will be charged the full consultation fee via your nominated payment method.

4

Receive your rebate

Our team will securely process the Medicare claim on your behalf on the same day. The rebate amount will usually appear in your bank account within 24-48 hours.

info

Important Process Details

  • Medicare does not cover cancellation fees. If you late-cancel or miss an appointment, you cannot claim a rebate for that fee.
  • You must ensure your Medicare details are up to date with Services Australia to receive automatic deposits.
  • Couples therapy and some specific assessments are generally not eligible for Medicare rebates under a standard MHCP.
